# Scout Fox Go™ — Kickstarter Master Plan (Summary for the AI)

Tagline: **Plan Less. Explore More.** Positioning: **The AI Decision Layer for
Human Experiences.** Founder & CEO: Meghan Suslak.

## Executive summary
Scout Fox Go is an AI planning companion for family travel and local experiences.
It sits one layer *above* booking sites — a decision layer that learns who is
traveling and returns a single good plan instead of a long list of options. The
Kickstarter funds finishing the recommendation engine, deepening booking
integrations, hardening security, and launching Scout Points.

## Problem & solution
Family travel planning falls on one person, drowning in options across many
sites, with generic recommendations that ignore real constraints (naps, ages,
budget, accessibility). Scout Fox Go understands the family and hands back a
bookable plan; the user adjusts it conversationally.

## The Decision Layer (core engine) — request flow
1. **Understand** — parse natural-language input into structured intent
   (destination, dates, budget, pace, who's coming, unspoken constraints).
2. **Gather** — pull live data from booking partners + context (timing, age-fit,
   accessibility, weather, location).
3. **Reason** — score/combine options against the family profile, balancing cost
   vs convenience, ambition vs realism.
4. **Compose** — assemble a coherent plan, structured by day and energy, each
   item tied to a visible reason.
5. **Refine** — conversational feedback reshapes the plan.
Design: modular layers (experience / decision / integration / data+identity);
improves with use; explainable recommendations.

## Product features
Family Profiles, Conversational Planning, Smart Itineraries (by day + energy),
Live Booking Connections, Scout Points Rewards, Local Experiences, Save/Share/
Decide Together.

## User personas
The Default Planner (often a parent/mom carrying it alone), The Time-Starved
Couple, The Multigenerational Organizer, The Local Explorer.

## Competitive map
Four existing tool types fail the decision: booking platforms (sell inventory),
search/review (more info), general AI assistants (no live inventory or persistent
profile), travel agents (don't scale). Scout Fox Go fills the gap: agent-level
judgment + platform reach + conversational ease, focused on families.

## Booking integrations
**Duffel** (flights), **Kayak** (breadth of search), **PHPtravels** (flexible
booking framework). Each sits behind a consistent internal interface so the
experience is seamless regardless of source. Philosophy: integrate, don't
rebuild inventory/pricing/payments.

## Scout Points (technical)
Central auditable ledger of earn/redeem events; configurable earning rules;
anti-abuse rate limits + validation; partner integrations. Earn: profile
completion, saving plans, booking, referrals, community. Redeem: early access,
upgrades, partner benefits, founding recognition.

## Security & legal
**FlowVault** = internal security standard: encryption in transit/at rest, data
minimization, access control, privacy by design, extra care for child data
(COPPA-minded). Legal/privacy framework built early; not legal advice.

## Accessibility
Accessible app (contrast, screen reader, clear language) AND accessible *plans*
(step-free routes, age limits, sensory/mobility considerations).

## Business model
Booking-driven revenue (partner economics) + premium membership + Scout Points/
partner ecosystem. Aligned (earn when families get value), diversified,
compounding.

## Reward tiers (proposed)
Scout Supporter $15 · Early Explorer $35 · Family Founder $75 · Trip Architect
$150 · Founding Circle $300 · Lifetime Scout $600.

## Stretch goals
Goal: core build. +25% local-experience planning · +50% deeper accessibility in
plans · +75% family sharing/group decisions · +100% expanded Scout Points catalog
+ more booking partners.

## Budget model
Product & engineering 40% · Booking integrations & data 15% · Security &
compliance 10% · Design & UX 10% · Marketing & community 12% · Scout Points &
rewards 5% · Operations & contingency 8%.

## 12-month roadmap
Foundation (M1-3): finish engine + integrations, founding-member early access.
Refine (M4-6): sharpen recs, harden security, launch Scout Points v1.
Expand (M7-9): local experiences, family sharing, broader booking, stretch goals.
Scale (M10-12): open beyond founding members, accessibility, premium + growth.

## Launch playbook
Full-funnel marketing (recognition → belief → action → belonging); parenting/
family-travel influencers; pre-launch + launch + mid + final-push email
sequences; warm/real/funny social; PR around launch + milestones; calm skimmable
Kickstarter page; feeling-first video; front-loaded day-by-day timeline.

## Team & ops
Founder-led, lean; own the decision layer / family experience / brand, partner
for inventory + specialized work. Continuous QA (accuracy, fit, reliability,
safety) with real founding families. Mostly-digital fulfillment; communication is
fulfillment.

## Risks & mitigations
Adoption (lead with recognition, build a loyal founding cohort), recommendation
quality (continuous testing, flag uncertainty), partner dependence (multiple
partners behind one interface), competition (focus + trust as the moat), data/
trust (FlowVault), execution/runway (lean, contingency, honest updates).

## Long-term vision
Beyond travel: the trusted decision layer for the hundreds of experience
decisions families make a year — the AI Decision Layer for Human Experiences.
